Admission Questions

When does the admission process start?

Admission for the next academic year typically starts in September of the previous year. For the 2026 academic year, applications will open on September 1st, 2025. We encourage parents to apply early as places fill up quickly, especially for popular classes like S.1 and S.5.

What are the age requirements for S.1?

For admission to Senior One (S.1), a student should be between 13 and 15 years old by December 31st of the admission year. For S.5, the age range is 17-19 years. Exceptions may be considered on a case-by-case basis.

Can I apply for transfer in the middle of the term?

While we prefer admissions at the beginning of a term, mid-term transfers may be accepted depending on space availability. However, the student must sit for placement exams and provide transfer letters from the previous school. Fees will be prorated accordingly.

Is there an entrance examination?

Yes, all new students (except those joining S.1 with Division 1 and S.5 with Division 1) are required to sit for entrance examinations in English, Mathematics, and General Paper. The exams are held every Saturday at 9:00 AM at the main campus. Please bring your own writing materials.

Fees & Payments

How much are school fees per term?

Fees vary by class and category (day vs. boarding). For O-Level (S.1-S.4): Day scholars pay UGX 350,600 (continuing) and UGX 411,600 (new), while boarders pay UGX 670,600 (continuing) and UGX 731,600 (new). For A-Level (S.5-S.6): Day scholars pay UGX 365,600 (continuing) and UGX 426,600 (new), while boarders pay UGX 685,600 (continuing) and UGX 746,600 (new). Please see our Fees Structure page for details.

What payment methods are accepted?

We accept payments through multiple channels:

  • Bank: Centenary Bank, Account: 3216549870
  • Mobile Money: MTN 0754645377, Airtel 0778654311
  • School Bursar's Office: Cash or cheque (receipt issued immediately)
Always request an official receipt for any payment made.

Is the uniform fee included in school fees?

Uniform fees are separate and paid once upon admission. For new students, uniform fees are: Boys UGX 140,000 (O-Level) / 120,000 (A-Level), Girls UGX 120,000, and Hostel Uniform UGX 40,000. Continuing students only pay for uniform if they need replacements.

Are there any discounts for siblings?

Yes, we offer sibling discounts: 15% for two siblings, 20% for three siblings, and 25% for four or more siblings. The discount is applied to the total fees before other scholarships. This does not apply to uniform fees.

What is the deadline for fee payment?

Fees should be paid in full on or before the opening day of each term. For Term I, payment is due on opening day. For Term II and III, payment should be made at least one week before the term begins. Late payment attracts a penalty of UGX 20,000 per week.

Academics

What subjects are offered at O-Level?

We offer a comprehensive range of subjects at O-Level including:

  • Sciences: Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry, Biology
  • Humanities: History, Geography, CRE, Literature
  • Languages: English, French, Kiswahili, Luganda
  • Vocational: Entrepreneurship, ICT, Art & Design, Technical Drawing, Physical Education
Students take a minimum of 8 and maximum of 10 subjects.

What A-Level combinations are available?

We offer both Science and Arts combinations:

  • Sciences: PCM (Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics), BCM (Biology, Chemistry, Mathematics), PCB (Physics, Chemistry, Biology), MCB (Mathematics, Chemistry, Biology)
  • Arts: HED (History, Economics, Divinity), LEG (Literature, Economics, Geography), HEG (History, Economics, Geography), FEG (French, Economics, Geography)
Selection is based on UCE results and subject grades.

When are the national examinations?

PLE (Primary Leaving Exam) is usually in November, UCE (O-Level) in October-November, and UACE (A-Level) in November-December. Specific dates are announced by UNEB (Uganda National Examinations Board) at the beginning of each year. Our Academic Calendar includes all exam dates.

Do you offer extra lessons or remedial classes?

Yes, we offer remedial classes for students who need extra help, especially in core subjects like Mathematics, English, and Sciences. These are conducted after regular school hours and on Saturdays. There is a small additional fee for remedial classes, but some are offered free of charge for struggling students.

Boarding Life

What are the dormitory arrangements?

We have separate dormitories for boys and girls, each supervised by resident wardens. Dormitories are spacious with adequate lighting and ventilation. Each student is provided with a bed, mattress, locker, and study desk. Students are grouped by age and class to ensure proper care and supervision.

What meals are provided for boarders?

Boarders receive three balanced meals daily: breakfast (tea with bread/mandazi/porridge), lunch (matooke/rice/posho with beans/meat/fish and vegetables), and supper (similar to lunch with variety). Evening tea is also provided at 4:30 PM. Special diets are catered for students with medical conditions upon notification.

Can parents visit their children during the term?

Yes, parents are welcome to visit on designated visiting days, which are usually the last Sunday of every month from 10:00 AM to 4:00 PM. For emergency visits, please contact the school administration in advance. During examination periods, visits may be restricted to allow students to concentrate.

What items should a boarder bring?

Boarders should bring:

  • Bed sheets (2 pairs), blanket, pillow
  • Towels, soap, toothpaste, toothbrush
  • Plate, cup, spoon, knife, fork
  • School uniform, P.E. kit, sweater, raincoat
  • Books, stationery, mathematical set
  • Torch, padlock, box/trunk
Prohibited items include phones, smart devices, expensive jewelry, and illegal substances.

Uniform & Dress Code

Where can I buy the school uniform?

School uniforms are available at the school's uniform shop, open Monday to Friday from 8:00 AM to 4:00 PM. You can also purchase from approved vendors in Mityana town. All uniforms must meet the school's specifications. New students receive their uniform after paying the uniform fee.

What is the complete uniform for boys and girls?

Boys: Khaki shorts (O-Level) / Grey trousers (A-Level), light blue shirt, school tie, black shoes, white socks, navy blue sweater (optional). Girls: Light blue dress (O-Level) / Navy blue skirt and light blue blouse (A-Level), school tie, black shoes, white socks, navy blue sweater (optional). P.E. Kit: White t-shirt, navy blue shorts, white canvas shoes. All uniforms must be clearly labeled with the student's name.

Are there specific requirements for hairstyle?

Yes, we maintain a neat and modest appearance. Boys must have short, well-groomed hair (no dreadlocks, braids, or dyed hair). Girls with natural hair must keep it neat and tidy; if relaxed, it should be of moderate length. Weaving, extensive braiding, and colored hair are not permitted. Extreme or trendy hairstyles are discouraged.

Sports & Co-curricular Activities

What sports are offered at the school?

We offer a wide range of sports including football (boys and girls), netball, basketball, volleyball, athletics (track and field), swimming, badminton, table tennis, and chess. Our students participate in inter-house, inter-school, and national competitions. We have professional coaches for each sport.

Are there clubs and societies students can join?

Yes, we have numerous clubs including: Debate Club, Science Club, Wildlife Club, Journalism Club, Drama Club, Music & Choir, Scripture Union, Scouts & Guides, Red Cross, and Rotaract. Students are encouraged to join at least one club to develop leadership and social skills.

Is participation in sports compulsory?

Yes, every student is required to participate in at least one sport or club activity. We believe in holistic education and developing both mind and body. Students with medical conditions that prevent physical activity are excused with a doctor's note and assigned alternative activities.

General Questions

What are the school hours?

School hours are from 8:00 AM to 4:30 PM, Monday to Friday. Lessons begin at 8:30 AM after assembly. There is a break from 10:30 AM to 11:00 AM and lunch from 1:00 PM to 2:00 PM. Day scholars should arrive by 7:45 AM and be picked up by 4:45 PM. Boarders follow a structured schedule including evening preps from 7:00 PM to 9:30 PM.

How does the school handle discipline?

We follow a restorative discipline approach focusing on positive reinforcement and character development. Minor offenses are handled through counseling and guidance by class teachers. Serious offenses are managed by the Discipline Committee following the school's code of conduct. Corporal punishment is not used; instead, we emphasize correction, counseling, and community service.

Is there a school clinic?

Yes, we have a well-equipped school clinic staffed by a qualified nurse and a visiting doctor. Minor ailments are treated at the clinic. For serious cases, students are referred to Mityana Hospital. Parents are contacted immediately in case of emergencies. Boarders contribute to a medical fund included in their fees.

How can I contact my child during school hours?

For emergencies, you can call the school office at 0754645377, and the message will be relayed to your child. Boarders have access to the school phone at designated times. Personal mobile phones are not allowed for students. Parents are encouraged to communicate through the class teacher or house warden.

Does the school offer transport services?

Yes, we have school buses that transport day scholars from designated pick-up points in Mityana town and surrounding areas. Transport is optional and costs an additional fee. Pick-up and drop-off schedules are shared at the beginning of each term. Boarders are transported to and from the bus park at the beginning and end of term.
